\n\tObservation number one, on strategy:\xc2\xa0 What we\'ve seen in this trip is the implementation of a substantial and important reorientation in American global strategy.\xc2\xa0 That is the rebalancing of our efforts towards the challenges and opportunities in Asia on the part of the United States.\xc2\xa0 Now -- and the President, I think, best summarized this in his Canberra speech before the Australian parliament, when he said the United States is all in.\xc2\xa0 And I think that\'s what you\'ve seen during the course of this trip.\xc2\xa0

\n

\n\tThis has been an effort that we\'ve had underway, as many of you know, since our transition into office.\xc2\xa0 During that transition, we had the opportunity to ask ourselves the following questions:\xc2\xa0 Where are the opportunities of the future?\xc2\xa0 Where are we currently, as the President comes into office, underweighted and overweighted with respect to U.S. effort?\xc2\xa0

\n

\n\tAnd the conclusion that we reached, among others, is that we were substantially underweighted in terms of our engagement and resource allocation and attention to Asia.\xc2\xa0 And we have been about a three-year effort to address that.\xc2\xa0 And we call this rebalancing -- strategic rebalancing, which I\'ve talked to a number of you about over the course of the last three years, as we\'ve laid this out and implemented this during the course of the President\'s three trips to Asia and many things in between, obviously.

\n

\n\tAgain, it was born of the observation that we were underweighted in Asia, given the importance of the region, given the economic dynamism in the region, and the strategic dynamics in the region.\xc2\xa0 And we set about, through three lines of quite specific work, to address that underweighting.\xc2\xa0 And they were these:\xc2\xa0 First and foremost, to strengthen our alliances and security partnerships in the region.\xc2\xa0

\n

\n\tAlliances are critical for the United States around the world.\xc2\xa0 Alliances are an essential strategic asset for the United States around the world.\xc2\xa0 They are very different than coalitions of the willing.\xc2\xa0 And they are to be valued by the United States\xc2\xa0 -- and we do that, and we\'ve done that in Europe, and we\'ve done that in Asia.\xc2\xa0 Indeed, over the last two weeks the United States has -- the President has met face-to-face with each of our Asia treaty allies, and engaged in really the continuing work to strengthen those alliances.\xc2\xa0
