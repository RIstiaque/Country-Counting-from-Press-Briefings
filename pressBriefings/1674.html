\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 SECRETARY GEITHNER:\xc2\xa0 I\'m just going to start with the broad imperative:\xc2\xa0 Economic recovery requires actions on three fronts.\xc2\xa0 The President signed into law yesterday this very powerful package of investments and tax incentives to help keep people working, help businesses stay operating.\xc2\xa0 This is a absolutely necessary part of recovery.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 As a complement to that, we\'re going to have to take further actions to strengthen our financial system, to make sure it\'s providing the credit necessary to spur recovery.\xc2\xa0 You\'re going to see additional details from us in the coming weeks on how we\'re going to do that -- again, how we\'re going to make banks strong enough they can support recovery, how we\'re going to provide direct support for the credit markets that are so important to small businesses lending, to consumer lending.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 These are necessary but not sufficient conditions to help address the housing crisis.\xc2\xa0 So the President is announcing today a very comprehensive plan of initiatives to help make housing more affordable and help, again, arrest this very damaging spiral we\'re seeing in the housing markets as a whole.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 This program has three important parts.\xc2\xa0 Let me just walk through these very quickly.\xc2\xa0 The first is a program to allow Americans who cannot take advantage of lower interest rates today to refinance.\xc2\xa0 Right now if you\'re a -- got a typical mortgage, but the value of your house price has declined, you\'re not able to take advantage of lower interest rates.\xc2\xa0 So this program, which we think will reach between -- could reach between 4 and 5 million Americans -- will help people take advantage of lower interest rates and, in that context, provide substantial reductions in monthly payments.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 The second program is a $75 billion program of incentives and measures to help improve affordability of mortgage payments for those families most at risk of foreclosure.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 My colleagues, Shaun Donovan and Sheila Bair will provide more details on that program.\xc2\xa0 You have a detailed fact sheet that shows how that works.\xc2\xa0 It\'s a combination of powerful incentives to lenders to participate and some conditions and other inducements to try to make it work.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 This will reach between 3 and 4 million Americans.\xc2\xa0 Again, the focus is on improving affordability in mortgage payments for people at risk of losing their home.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 The third important part is some additional financial support to Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac.\xc2\xa0 These two institutions, in effect, are the mortgage market today.\xc2\xa0 They account for the vast bulk of mortgages originated today.\xc2\xa0 They play a critical role in these markets, and we need to make sure that they have the ability to play that role going forward.\xc2\xa0 And so we\'ve increased substantially, using the authority Congress provided the administration last summer, to increase our financial commitment.\xc2\xa0 This will be important to helping keep mortgage rates down and is a very critical, important part of recovery.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 This is -- all Americans have a stake in making this work, not just those Americans who were the victim of bad underwriting standards, or in communities where you\'ve seen -- foreclosure.\xc2\xa0 It has broad-based effects, again, by helping Americans take advantage of lower interest rates, and helping improve affordability, helping keep overall mortgage rates low.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 Important to just emphasize the way this affects the economy as a whole:\xc2\xa0 Two important effects -- again, by helping keep mortgage rates down and helping reduce monthly payments, you\'re putting money in the hands of Americans.\xc2\xa0 In that case, it acts like stimulus.\xc2\xa0 Second is we\'re going to help reduce the risk that housing prices fall more than they would otherwise fall.\xc2\xa0 So again, by keeping interest rates low, by making it more affordable for people to stay in their homes, and by reducing the amount of foreclosures ahead, we can reduce the risk that housing prices fall further than they otherwise would.\xc2\xa0 Those two -- for those two reasons, again, all Americans have a stake in making this work.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 This is necessary policy.\xc2\xa0 It\'s smart economics.\xc2\xa0 And it\'s just and fair -- because Americans across the country that were responsible in how much they borrowed are being significantly damaged by the actions of those who are less responsible, both people who borrowed more than they could afford, and banks that took risks they didn\'t understand and could not sustain.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 I just want to end by complimenting Shaun Donovan, Sheila Bair, and a range of people across the administration who helped design this.\xc2\xa0 They both have enormous credibility in this area.\xc2\xa0 Sheila, I just want to say, was an early, powerful, pragmatic, creative advocate of action on the housing front.\xc2\xa0 And I\'m very pleased she was able to join the President and Shaun and I here today.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 Thank you.\xc2\xa0 Shaun, do you want to add anything?
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 SECRETARY DONOVAN:\xc2\xa0 Thank you, Tim.\xc2\xa0 As Tim has said, this is critical -- the announcement today is critical to getting the American economy back on the right path.\xc2\xa0 And let\'s be clear, housing has been a significant part of initiating the economic slide that we\'re in, and will be a key part of getting us out.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 Close to 10 percent of all American families today are either in foreclosure or behind on their mortgages.\xc2\xa0 And to dramatize how important this is to the continuing slide in home prices that we see, estimates are that in December, 45 percent of all home sales were distressed sales -- 45 percent of all sales were distressed sales -- which is continuing to drive prices lower.\xc2\xa0 So we have to get out of that spiral.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 Here in Arizona, over 6 percent of all mortgages are more than 90 days late or in foreclosure -- so a crucial problem to be solved here, as well as across the country.\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 With the first two pieces of the announcement that Secretary Geithner has talked about, between our refinancing initiative -- or so-called "underwater" borrowers -- and our modification plan, mortgage modification plan, we will reach between -- up to 7 to 9 million American families.\xc2\xa0 This is a smart, targeted investment which can reach and help to make more affordable more than $1.5 trillion of mortgage debt.\xc2\xa0 Those 7 to 9 million families hold roughly over $1.5 trillion in mortgage debt.\xc2\xa0 So we just have a scale that can have a real impact on turning the housing problems around in this country.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 First of all, to focus on the refinancing portion, this will be focused on up to 4 to 5 million homeowners who have played by the rules, that have been making their payments on time.\xc2\xa0 These families have seen, through no fault of their own, values in their communities on houses drop by 20, 30, 40, even 50 percent, and find themselves in a situation where even if they\'re holding a mortgage that is far above market rates, they cannot take advantage of refinancing down to what are really historically low mortgage rates that we see in the market today.\xc2\xa0 This just isn\'t fair, and it\'s something that we will help to fix through the announcement today.\xc2\xa0
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 The typical family that has one of these so-called conforming mortgages, or Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac mortgages, that can re-fi to today\'s markets rates will save roughly $2,300 a year in lower interest payments, simply by refinancing to today\'s market rates.\xc2\xa0 And let me be clear:\xc2\xa0 These families have played by the rules; they\'re families that only have been victims of their houses falling in value and, therefore, their mortgages are close to or higher than the value of their homes today.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 So that\'s the first.\xc2\xa0 The second is a mortgage modification plan, in many ways, based on the pioneering work that Sheila Bair did at FDIC, to show that a streamlined, large-scale modification plan can have major impacts.\xc2\xa0 It will reach between, we believe, up to 3 to 4 million American families.\xc2\xa0 Let\'s be clear about the scale here.\xc2\xa0 Our expectation is that -- we saw 2.2 million foreclosures, a record number, last year.\xc2\xa0 We expect to see as many as 6 million foreclosures in the coming years.\xc2\xa0 So between these two initiatives, reaching 7 to 9 million families is at a scale that can really begin to turn this problem around.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 The modification program -- first of all, we must be clear that while this crisis in housing started as a mortgage crisis, in many ways, it has become a job-loss crisis, as well.\xc2\xa0 And so the signing yesterday of the recovery plan, which will create about 3.5 million jobs, is a critical piece of getting housing back on track by getting people back to work.\xc2\xa0 But beyond that, this modification plan does a number of things to make sure that up to 3 to 4 million families can stay in their homes and have affordable mortgages.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 First of all, it focuses on the right people:\xc2\xa0 investor-owners, flippers, speculators will not be eligible for the program; only owner occupants.\xc2\xa0 Second of all -- and this is something that is I think very innovative about this plan -- is that we are saying for the first time that any borrower that is current -- you don\'t have to be delinquent to qualify for this plan.\xc2\xa0 This is different from what\'s been done in the past.\xc2\xa0 What we\'ve found in our research is that the earlier we can get to homeowners that are in trouble, the more chance they have at successfully modifying their mortgage and being homeowners in the long term.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 And so we are providing new, innovative incentives for servicers to modify mortgages where people are current, where they haven\'t reached the state of 60 or 90 days delinquent, when they\'re more likely to fail.\xc2\xa0 And those again are the most deserving borrowers because they played by the rules, they struggled to make payments, but they have made those payments and they haven\'t been able to benefit from modification programs before.\xc2\xa0 So we are changing that.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 Finally, we will provide a series of incentive payments for success both for owners where you can benefit from a $1,000-a-year payment up to five years -- if you\'re successful and succeed in your modification for up to five years, $5,000 that can reduce the principal on your home mortgage -- as well as incentive payments to servicers and lenders that if the modifications work over the years we will make payments to them as incentives to keep people current.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 And in all, this will help, as I said, 3 to 4 million families.\xc2\xa0 But let\'s be clear:\xc2\xa0 This will also help millions of other families, as well.\xc2\xa0 Recent research shows that neighboring homes to foreclosed homes lose as much as 9 percent of their value.\xc2\xa0 So people who are not in danger of foreclosure still are suffering from nearby foreclosures.\xc2\xa0 This will help those families, as well.\xc2\xa0 Our estimates are that the average home -- not the average home in foreclosure, but the average home across the country will gain $6,000 in value relative to had this plan not been put in place.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 And finally, the third leg of this plan which Tim has talked about, keeping the GSEs providing low interest mortgages is absolutely key.\xc2\xa0 Together with FHA they represent more than 90 percent of the mortgage market today, all new mortgages issued. Combined with the $8,000 first-time homebuyer tax credit that was in the recovery bill signed yesterday, we believe these efforts can help to prop up the housing market and return it to stability.
\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 Thank you.

\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0 So that missing link is being filled today in a way that I think is effective and responsible.\xc2\xa0 It\'s a program of shared responsibility, looking to servicers, investors, borrowers, as well as the government, to all work together and make a contribution to get these loans restructured.\xc2\xa0
