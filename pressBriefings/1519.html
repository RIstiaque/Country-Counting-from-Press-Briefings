Q\xc2\xa0So what do you think you\'ve accomplished this trip on health care reform?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 Look, again, I think these are excellent opportunities to talk to people about what is involved in health care reform and what isn\xe2\x80\x99t involved in health care reform.\xc2\xa0 I think the President addressed both of those subjects today and in the Montana town hall meeting.\xc2\xa0 I think we\'ve proved we can have a civil town hall meeting.\xc2\xa0 I\'d point out that Grand Junction is a place where we got about 35 percent of the vote out of 100, so it\'s not exactly Austin, Texas.\xc2\xa0 So I think it proves that people want to have a genuine discussion about the issues.\xc2\xa0 I know the President was happy with both events and felt like it gave him an opportunity to talk about what\'s at stake.
Q\xc2\xa0It seemed like, when the President invoked his grandmother\'s story at this past town hall, that he really sort of matched the emotion more than he had previously in some of the protesters that we\'ve seen.\xc2\xa0 Can you talk a little bit about that approach, that strategy?\xc2\xa0 Why hadn\xe2\x80\x99t he come out with that kind of emotion earlier?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 I don\'t -- look, obviously there has been a tremendous amount of misinformation.\xc2\xa0 I\'m sure some of it is logically explained.\xc2\xa0 I think some of it is, as I\'ve said many times before, perpetuated on purpose despite the fact that people know the truth by people who ought to be smarter than that.\xc2\xa0
I think the President invoked the image because, as he said in the answer, it\'s ludicrous to think somebody who had struggled with losing a grandparent, one that meant so much to his development and upbringing, only a day before -- only hours before he\'s elected President -- the notion that he would then go around proposing something as has been discussed by people who know better is crazy.\xc2\xa0 So I think it gave him another opportunity to discuss it.
Q\xc2\xa0Can we expect to see the President on the road even more in September pushing health care?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 I think he\'ll spend a decent amount of time on the road, yes.
Q\xc2\xa0Looking towards the VFW speech on Monday, how much of a component of this speech will the Afghan strategy be?\xc2\xa0 It seems that -- will he be talking to this group about the drawing down of Iraq troops and increasing U.S. presence in --
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 Well, he\'s certainly going to talk about the commitments that the men and women of our Armed Forces have made in Iraq and Afghanistan, their contributions to the betterment of those two countries.\xc2\xa0 He\'ll obviously touch on what we\'re accomplishing, what we hope to get out -- what we hope to accomplish.\xc2\xa0 He won\'t get into specifics beyond the -- as you know, General McChrystal is working on a plan that will be sent to the President after the elections that, as Secretary Gates said, will not include troop adjustments in that report.\xc2\xa0 But he\'ll talk about where we are currently in both those two conflicts.\xc2\xa0 He\'ll talk about what we owe the men and women in uniform, and talk about the choices that we\'ve had to make over the past few months about precious resources in our budget and whether or not we\'re going to fund expensive weapons programs the Pentagon says we don\'t need, or give our men and women fighting in those two dangerous places in the world and other places the resources they need.
Q\xc2\xa0On Afghanistan, will he be talking about the strategy overall, whether it\'s working or not, whether -- what you see going forward?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 I don\'t expect the President is going to lay out a detailed sort of where we are in Afghanistan, but certainly a little bit -- a less technical update on what\'s happening.
Q\xc2\xa0Will he talk about the election there this coming week, or will he just want to stay clear of that to not seem to be interfering?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 The latest draft I saw did not have him getting a ton into that.\xc2\xa0 Obviously it\'s something that we and the rest of the world are watching and will watch.
Q\xc2\xa0Did you guys put anything out today on the blast right outside the -- why am I blanking here --
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 On the embassies --
Q\xc2\xa0-- right, the embassies and NATO?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 No.\xc2\xa0 I mean, obviously the President is aware of the situation.\xc2\xa0 Look, I think, as many of our folks have said -- General McChrystal and others have said -- and you\'ve seen what the Taliban has said -- they want very much to disrupt the first election that will be conducted by the Afghan people.\xc2\xa0 And we will -- obviously we\'ve got tremendous resources there to ensure that there\'s a security situation that allows the Afghans to choose their leaders and we hope and expect to see a good, free and fair election and one that hopefully comes off without violence and bloodshed.
Q\xc2\xa0On Monday -- can you give us a week-ahead before he goes on vacation?\xc2\xa0 What\'s he got planned next week?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 Well, we did a little on Friday.\xc2\xa0 I\'ll do some of this off the top of my head.\xc2\xa0 Tuesday I believe Mubarak is in town from Egypt.\xc2\xa0 Wednesday is the rescheduled NASCAR event.\xc2\xa0 Thursday he\'s at the White House.\xc2\xa0 And at some point on Friday he\'ll leave to go to Camp David.
Q\xc2\xa0On a lighter note, how do you think Obama is doing in terms of being kind of an outdoorsy guy this weekend?\xc2\xa0
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 Well, I know he had a tremendous amount of fun at Yellow Stone.\xc2\xa0 You know, Governor Schweitzer from Montana had told him he\'ll never -- he said, you\'ll never forget the look on your girls\' face when they see Old Faithful go off.\xc2\xa0 And so I think he was looking forward to that.\xc2\xa0
Obviously -- we were talking about this on the drive back -- and he remembers coming -- when he was 11, his first trip to the mainland, they went to -- his family went to Yellow Stone, they went to the Grand Canyon.\xc2\xa0 So in a number of ways, I think he wants very much to see and share the outdoors and some of the beautiful places in the country with his daughters.\xc2\xa0
Q\xc2\xa0Was Maya there also?

Q\xc2\xa0He told Letterman last September that she was there and so was his grandmother and his mother.
Q\xc2\xa0It\'s been interesting to me to watch him, not being from the great American West, and how much time he\'s been spending at Camp David -- do you think the family in general has developed more of an appreciation for -- I don\'t know -- the outdoors way of life, hiking?
MR. GIBBS:\xc2\xa0 Well, look, I think he\'s always tremendously enjoyed being outside with his family.\xc2\xa0 I mean, I can remember him telling stories about -- during the campaign and in the months before the campaign, the summer before the campaign, one of the things he said he would miss the most was riding up and down Lake Shore with his girls on their bikes.\xc2\xa0 Look, he was dying to come here and go fishing.\xc2\xa0 Literally, we -- every time we\'d been to Montana -- the primary, the general election -- every time we went he said, I\'m going to come back here and learn how to fly fish.\xc2\xa0 He was dying to do that, and finally got a chance to do that, though was a bit frustrated he didn\xe2\x80\x99t get to hold one of the fish.
